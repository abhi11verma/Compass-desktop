More features isn't the answer.
More features is the problem.

We live in an age of infinite inputs. More tools, more tabs, more systems. You sit down with a spark — an idea, a project, a habit you want to build — and two days later it's gone, buried under new urgencies and noise.

Compass is something else entirely.

A personal life dashboard — a post-it note, not a filing cabinet. It holds the things you've chosen to care about right now, and hides everything else. When the noise gets loud and you lose your footing, you open Compass. It points you back.

Opinionated by design.

Compass restricts you. Deliberately.

You get a small number of active Focuses — the things commanding your attention right now, each with a clear process for how you'll work on them. You get Habits — small daily behaviours compounding beneath the surface. And you get Values and Principles — your anchor when everything feels urgent.

The interface only shows what's in focus. Everything else is hidden. No infinite views, no plugin ecosystem, no customisation rabbit holes. The constraint is the point.

Focuses What you're working on right now.
Habits The behaviours you're building daily.
Values & Principles Who you are when it counts.

This will work for some people. It won't work for everyone. That's intentional.

⚠
macOS — First launch

Compass is free and open-source. Apple requires a $99/yr developer certificate to silently trust apps — we skip that so the app stays free. macOS will block the first launch with a "cannot be opened" warning. Here's how to get past it in seconds:

01 Right-click the Compass app in your Applications folder (or wherever you placed it)
02 Choose Open from the context menu — not double-click
03 A dialog appears asking to confirm. Click Open once more
04 macOS remembers your choice — every launch after this is normal

Works in your browser. Your data lives in localStorage — private by default.
